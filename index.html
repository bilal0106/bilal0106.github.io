Master's Thesis

Machine Learning Approach for Cluster Head Selection in Internet of Things-Based Wireless Sensor Network

My master's thesis focuses on optimizing Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs) in Internet of Things (IoT) environments through intelligent cluster head selection using machine learning algorithms. In IoT-based WSNs, efficient clustering and selecting optimal cluster heads is crucial for extending network lifetime and enhancing overall performance.

The research proposes a novel machine learning approach that considers multiple parameters such as residual energy, node centrality, communication cost, and node density to make optimal decisions about cluster head selection. By implementing predictive models that adapt to network conditions, the approach significantly improves energy efficiency and data transmission reliability.

The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed machine learning-based approach outperforms traditional clustering algorithms, extending network lifetime by up to 25% while maintaining high quality of service metrics. This research contributes to the ongoing efforts to make IoT networks more sustainable and reliable for various applications including environmental monitoring, smart cities, and industrial automation.

Research Highlights
  • Novel ML-based cluster head selection algorithm
  • 25% improvement in network lifetime
  • Enhanced energy efficiency across heterogeneous IoT devices
  • Adaptive to changing network conditions
  • Reduced communication overhead
